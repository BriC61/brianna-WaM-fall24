"I got addicted to gua bao when I was 10 years old. I joined my dad for a one day business trip to a place that happened to be famous for its gua bao. My dad bought one for me to try, and it was so delicious that, even today, I still can't forget how it looked and tasted."

Edwina, April 2020

"As a RISD student in the 50’s, I remember walking up to Toy Sun’s, Thayer St., East Side of Providence for a delicious late night snack/study break… a chow mein sandwich… on a hamburger roll! Invented in Fall River, btw. The price was right, too!"

Joanne Egan, Oct 2024

"It has been nearly 5 years since I've had my poh poh's 稀飯. My brother thinks about it nearly every time we talk about our grandparents, but I could never stand the fishy smell of the squid with cilantro"

"My family went to Mee Hongs every Sunday growing up. We were waited on by a man named Chen, he was a friend of my Uncle. When I was young I did not like most food but he always brought me Veal cutlet, brown gravy, white rice and French fries. Almond cookie for dessert. As I got older it was shrimp and chicken chow mein, fried rice and tea."

Dennis, Sept 2013

"I used to be completely hamburgers-and-pizza as a kid. But when I went off to college, suddenly all I craved were rice and noodles. As soon as I landed home from college, I made my parents stop at a chinese restaurant on the way so we could eat beef chow fun."

"When I was a young boy, my mother took me shopping with her and promised if I behaved she would take me to Mee Hong for chow mien. To this day it was my favorite and I have searched for years (I’m 70) to find a place that makes the same recipe with no luck."

Richard, Feb 2013

"I was a waitress at the Ming while I was in art school. I used to sit with “Charlie” at the front door and used to pick up his dry cleaning for him. Often he would have the remains of Ming wings in the pockets of his suit jackets :)"

Joanne M, March 2024

"My dad was from New York and my mom from Los Angeles where good Chinese restaurants were plentiful. They considered Luke’s to be the best, most authentic (New York and California style) Chinese place outside of Boston. Some of my earliest memories are of Luke’s (big fish tank, I think?) and their eggrolls and lobster Cantonese. They always gave my brother and me the little paper parasols to take home and play with."

Marya, Sept 2013

"Bob Tow ran the restaurant after his father died, and introduced Dim Sum to Providence before anyone who was not Chinese in the state even knew what it was."

Kenn, Jan 2009

"Henry Luke was my uncle. My parents met there as my dad was a regular, and my mom of course worked there growing up. My mom still cooks lobster Cantonese, and it’s still delicious. "

Rebecca, Oct 2016

"When we were ordering Chinese take out for the first time in a while, my mom and I were arguing over which type of sweet and sour pork to get. I didn't even know there were multiple kinds! I just thought there was the one with the artificially bright red sauce that only I liked. Apparently my mom also liked sweet and sour pork, just not that kind."

"Sometime the english translations of Chinese foods can be quite liberal... I'd always have to ask my mom to read the Chinese for me to check if I was ordering the right thing."
